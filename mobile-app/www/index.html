How it works

1

Upload your CSV

Your historical data — one row per event (sale, visit, order), one column per variable. Include the outcome you want to predict in one column.

2

5 models train automatically

Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, and LightGBM all train in parallel. The best one is crowned champion on the leaderboard.

3

Get your report

Feature importance tells you why the model predicts what it does. Download a full HTML or PDF report to share with anyone.

What your CSV should look like

One row per historical event (sale, visit, order, check-in…). One column per variable you track. The last column (or any column) should be the outcome you want to predict — like 1/0, yes/no, or a category.

Example — Gym member drop-off prediction

member_id*checkin_hourgap_daysavg_duration_mintrainer_visitsclasses_attendeddropped_off
M0017248350
M002191422011
M00361658120
… 100+ rows …

* ID columns (member_id, order_id etc.) are automatically detected and removed — they're not useful for prediction.
* Date columns are automatically parsed into day/month/year numbers.
* Missing values are filled automatically (median for numbers, most frequent for categories).
